    Our Humble Beginnings

    The IoT Initiative is a website designed to help foster the growth and development of the IoT architecture through various events, meetups, interactive labs and tutorials.

  • What is IoT?

    An Agency is Born

    IoT, or “The Internet of Things”, is the cross-connection of physical devices with one another through internet connectivity, allowing for the transfer of raw data amongst themselves. With these technologies, electronics, sensors, actuators, etc. are able to bridge the gap between the physical world and technology-based systems. This connection allows for better economic efficiency, reduced human interaction, and a more thorough understanding of how we interact with one another through our environments.

  • Consumer Applications

    Transition to Full Service

    Under the branding of “Consumer Applications”, the biggest name synonymous is the marketable term of a “Smart Home”. These devices are directly connected through one’s home network infrastructure, and end users are permitted to monitor/adjust temperature, modify lighting, interact with artificial intelligence (AI), as well as ensure appropriate dwelling security.

  • Commercial Applications

    Phase Two Expansion

    With the ever-evolving need of technology in the workforce, IoT stretches itself into the fields of medical and healthcare sectors, while also taking stakes in transportation, building, and home automation across various levels. In many commercial cases, sensors may be utilized to monitor the status of energy-based systems, communicating back to a centrally contained machine.

  • Industrial Applications

    Phase Three Expansion

    For industrial-based ventures, consider the breadth of manufacturing done to sustain the population of today. Many of these processes would not be possible without the involvement of computing systems through IoT. Some areas of interest under this emphasis include manufacturing and agricultural developments.

  • Infrastructural Applications

    Phase Four Expansion

    Similar to the industrial applications of IoT, an infrastructure-based approach would include the deployment of equipment on a metropolitan level, management of energy consumption, and the observance of environmental inhibitors/factors.
